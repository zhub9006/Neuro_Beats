# Neurologic Music Therapy (NMT) Protocol for Stroke Rehabilitation
## Glen Campbell-Informed Session Notes & Clinical Evidence Integration

---

## 1. Protocol Overview

This document provides a structured neurologic music therapy (NMT) protocol for stroke rehabilitation, integrating evidence from ClinicalTrials.gov studies with session design inspired by the legacy of **Glen Campbell** — whose public journey with Alzheimer's disease demonstrated the profound resilience of musical cognition in the face of neurological decline.

**Protocol Name:** NMT-Stroke-GCB (Neurologic Music Therapy for Stroke — Glen Campbell Bridge)
**Target Population:** Post-stroke patients with hemiparesis, aphasia, or gait impairment
**Implementation Platform:** NeuroBeat (https://github.com/Luci-699/Neuro_Beats)

## 2. Clinical Evidence Base (ClinicalTrials.gov)

The following registered clinical trials underpin this protocol. A total of **69 studies** were identified on ClinicalTrials.gov matching "music therapy stroke rehabilitation."

### 2.1 Trend Analysis Summary

| Dimension | Breakdown |
|-----------|-----------|
| **By Phase** | NA (not applicable/pharmacological): 66 studies; Phase 2: 2; Phase 4: 1 |
| **By Status** | Completed: 19; Recruiting: 21; Unknown: 12; Terminated: 7; Active Not Recruiting: 5; Not Yet Recruiting: 5 |
| **By Sponsor Type** | Other (academic/hospital/NGO): 68; Other-Gov: 1 |

**Key Insight:** The field is overwhelmingly driven by academic and hospital-based sponsors (not industry), with most trials classified as NA-phase (behavioral/non-drug interventions). Nearly one-third are actively recruiting, signaling growing momentum.

### 2.2 Key Referenced Trials

| NCT ID | Title | Key Finding / Relevance |
|--------|-------|------------------------|
| **NCT03306797** | SONICHAND — Music Therapy for Rehabilitation in Stroke Patients (Sonification + Leap Motion) | Sonification technique combined with hand-tracking sensor improved Fugl-Meyer upper limb scores vs. standard rehab in 65 stroke patients |
| **NCT02208219** | NEUROMUSIC — Music Therapy to Restore Motor Deficits After Stroke | Music-supported therapy (MST) using MIDI-piano and electronic drum improved upper limb function vs. conventional treatment; neuroimaging showed sensorimotor cortex plasticity |
| **NCT02310438** | TIMPStro — Music Therapy for Upper Limb Rehabilitation in Stroke Patients | Home-based Therapeutic Instrumental Music Performance (TIMP) improved ARAT scores; 30 min × 2/week × 6 weeks feasible and effective |
| **NCT03434496** | Music Role in PD Rehabilitation (RAS + treadmill) | Rhythmic Auditory Stimulation (RAS) improved gait parameters and sensorimotor connectivity (EEG) in Parkinson's — transferable rationale to stroke gait rehab |
| **NCT06761638** | Comparative Impact of RAST and TST for Improving Motor Functions in Stroke | RAS therapy vs. Task-Specific Training in 40 stroke patients; RAS improved Modified Barthel Index and upper limb ROM |
| **NCT06368323** | Community Choir Singing for Chronic Post-Stroke Aphasia | 12-week choir intervention improved functional communication, language recovery, and psychosocial outcomes in aphasia |
| **NCT06920524** | MUSIBOT — Musical Intervention for Stress During Botulinum Toxin Injections | Music reduced HRV-based stress markers during painful spasticity treatment in stroke/MS patients |

## 4. Glen Campbell Song-Based Session Design

Glen Campbell's songs are selected for their **tempo suitability**, **emotional resonance**, and **cultural familiarity** — critical factors in NMT engagement and adherence.

### 4.1 Song Registry

| Song | Tempo (BPM) | NMT Application | Emotional Theme |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Wichita Lineman** | ~76 | RAS gait training (moderate cadence); Speech rhythm | Perseverance, duty — mirrors rehabilitation persistence |
| **Rhinestone Cowboy** | ~104 | TIMP upper limb training; RAS gait (higher cadence); Cognitive rhythm | Aspiration, resilience — mirrors rehabilitation goals |
| **Gentle on My Mind** | ~92 | MIT for aphasia; Anxiety reduction; Balance training | Comfort, steadiness — calming anchor for stressful sessions |

### 4.2 BPM Adaptation Guidelines (per NeuroBeat platform)

| Impairment Level | Wichita Lineman (76 BPM) | Gentle on My Mind (92 BPM) | Rhinestone Cowboy (104 BPM) |
|-----------------|--------------------------|----------------------------|------------------------------|
| Severe | 40 BPM (halved) | 50 BPM | 60 BPM |
| Moderate | 50 BPM | 65 BPM | 80 BPM |
| Mild | 76 BPM (original) | 92 BPM (original) | 104 BPM (original) |

## 5. Structured Session Protocol

### Session Format (30 minutes)

| Phase | Duration | Activity |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Warm-Up** | 5 min | Vocal warm-up / gentle rhythmic tapping to song intro |
| **Core Therapy** | 20 min | Targeted NMT technique with song-based rhythmic cue |
| **Cool-Down / Reflection** | 5 min | Song listening for emotional regulation; patient self-report |

### Session Schedule (6-week program)

| Week | Session 1 | Session 2 |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | Wichita Lineman — RAS Gait Training | Gentle on My Mind — MIT Speech Training |
| 2 | Rhinestone Cowboy — TIMP Upper Limb | Wichita Lineman — Speech Rhythm |
| 3 | Gentle on My Mind — RAS Gait (moderate) | Rhinestone Cowboy — Cognitive Rhythm |
| 4 | Wichita Lineman — TIMP Upper Limb | Gentle on My Mind — Anxiety Reduction / Balance |
| 5 | Rhinestone Cowboy — RAS Gait (higher) | Wichita Lineman — RAS Gait (moderate) |
| 6 | Gentle on My Mind — MIT Review | Rhinestone Cowboy — TIMP Review + Celebration |
